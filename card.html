Giant Panda

The Giant Panda lives in mountainous regions, like Sichuan and Tibet. These magnificent mammals are the symbol of the World Wildlife Fund (WWF), a conservation organization.

  • Scientific name: Ailuropoda melanoleuca
  • Habitat: Bamboo Forest in Western China
  • Lifespan: 20 years
  • Height: 60 – 90 cm

Whilst they will occasionally eat small animals and fish, bamboo counts for 99 percent of their diet. Unlike most other bears, pandas do not hibernate. Female pandas give birth to one or two cubs every two years.

Sadly, these beautiful bears are endangered, and it’s estimated that only around 1,000 remain in the wild. That’s why we need to do all we can to protect them!
